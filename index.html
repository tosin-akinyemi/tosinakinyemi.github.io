Biography

About Me

I am Esther Akinyemi — a Healthcare Compliance Specialist and Project Coordinator based in Toronto, Ontario. With over five years of experience across telecom, hospitality, and technology sectors, I specialize in driving customer success, managing complex compliance frameworks, and coordinating cross-functional teams to deliver measurable results.

I am currently pivoting into healthcare compliance — leveraging deep expertise in HIPAA, PIPEDA, and GCP cloud security to help healthcare organizations build robust, ethical, and secure compliance frameworks that enable rather than restrict innovation.

My academic background includes a Master's in Informatics with a mixed concentration in Cloud Computing and Information Security from Northeastern University, a Diploma in Digital Business Management from Toronto School of Management, and a Bachelor's in Sociology and Anthropology.

Keybrew Lockdown — All 7 Slides

Keybrew Lockdown — Cybersecurity Program

Designed a full credential-attack defense program for a 20-store coffee chain facing $2.8M in losses from POS fraud. Mapped the NIST framework (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover) to a $110K pilot with a projected $900K annual savings, covering fingerprint POS login, CrowdStrike threat detection, and 90-second lockdown protocols.

Security Attack Report — Habib Bank Ransomware

Analysed a real-world ransomware attack on Habib Bank, tracing the full attack chain from phishing-based initial access through lateral movement, data exfiltration, and ransom demand. Produced remediation recommendations including MFA enforcement, network segmentation, patch management, and real-time anomaly monitoring.

Log Analytics — Azure Monitor & KQL

Used Azure Log Analytics with KQL (Kusto Query Language) to filter, summarise, and visualise log data. Queried the StormEvents dataset by state and severity level, grouped results using the summarize operator, and created bar charts and time-series visuals to surface security patterns and anomalies for compliance reporting.

Reflection

These assignments connected classroom theory directly to real security incidents and tools. Analysing the Habib Bank breach showed how fast a single stolen credential can cascade into a multi-million dollar loss. The Keybrew project pushed me to think like a security consultant — balancing risk, budget, and operational realities to build a defensible program that a business would actually adopt.
